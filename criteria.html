Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Complete HR Compliance Guide

Eligibility | Calculation | Exclusions | Code on Wages Clarification

1. Applicability

  • Applicable to factories, mines, oilfields, plantations, ports, railways
  • Shops & establishments employing 10 or more employees
  • Once covered, continues even if employee count falls below 10

2. Key Definitions

Employee

Any person employed for wages (excluding apprentices) in skilled, unskilled, supervisory, managerial, technical, or clerical roles.

Continuous Service

  • Worked 240 days in preceding 12 months (190 days for mines below ground)
  • Includes paid leave, maternity leave, sickness, accident, layoff

Wages (For Calculation)

Includes: Basic + Dearness Allowance

Excludes: HRA, Bonus, Commission, Overtime, Allowances

3. Eligibility for Gratuity

Eligible after completion of 4 years and 240 days of continuous service.

Gratuity Payable On:

  • Superannuation
  • Retirement
  • Resignation
  • Termination (except misconduct cases)
  • Death of employee (No minimum service required)
  • Permanent disablement (No minimum service required)

Special Note:

In case of death or permanent disablement, gratuity becomes payable irrespective of completion of 5 years.

4. Calculation of Gratuity

Formula:

(Last Drawn Salary × 15 × Years of Service) ÷ 26

Example

Basic + DA = ₹50,000
Service = 10 years

Gratuity = (50,000 × 15 × 10) ÷ 26 = ₹2,88,462

Rounding Rule

  • Above 6 months → Rounded to next year
  • Less than 6 months → Ignored

Maximum Limit

₹20,00,000

5. Forfeiture of Gratuity

  • Damage/loss caused to employer (to extent of damage)
  • Riotous or disorderly conduct
  • Act of violence
  • Offence involving moral turpitude during employment

6. Nomination

  • Employee must submit nomination after 1 year of service
  • In case of death, amount paid to nominee/legal heir

7. Payment & Compliance

  • Payment within 30 days of becoming payable
  • Interest applicable for delay
  • Maintain records and display notices
  • Issue calculation sheet to employee

8. Tax Treatment

  • Government employees – Fully exempt
  • Private employees – Exempt up to ₹20,00,000 or calculated limit (whichever is lower)

9. Fixed Term Employees

Eligible for gratuity upon completion of contract period even if service is less than 5 years.

10. Code on Wages – Clarification & Misconception

Common Misconception: Gratuity becomes applicable after 1 year under Code on Wages.

Reality:

  • Code on Wages does NOT change gratuity eligibility to 1 year.
  • Gratuity eligibility remains governed by the Gratuity Act provisions.
  • 4 years + 240 days rule (judicial interpretation) continues.
  • 1-year rule applies only for nomination submission, not eligibility.

Impact of Wage Definition Under New Codes:

  • Uniform wage definition across labour codes
  • Basic wage component may increase
  • Higher basic wage = Higher gratuity liability
